California Roll

Description

A California roll is a type of sushi roll that originated in North America and is a variation of the traditional Japanese sushi rolls. It is made with sushi rice, cucumber, imitation crab, and avocado, all wrapped in a sheet of nori (seaweed). The roll is often prepared as an uramaki, with the rice on the outside and the nori on the inside, giving it a visually distinct appearance. Sometimes, the outer layer of rice is sprinkled with toasted sesame seeds or roe, such as tobiko from flying fish, to enhance its flavor and appearance.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups uncooked white rice
  • 0.5 cup seasoned rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 0.25 pound cooked crab meat
  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise
  • 5 sheets nori (dry seaweed)
  • 1 avocado
  • 0.25 cup red caviar, such as tobiko
  • 1 English cucumber: (seeded and sliced into strips)
  • 2 tablespoons drained pickled ginger: (for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce: (30 ml)
  • 1 tablespoon wasabi paste: (15 ml)
